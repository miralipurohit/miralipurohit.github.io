I am a Ph.D. student at Arizona State University (ASU). I am working alongside Dr. Hannah Kerner. I have closely collaborated with researchers at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) and Fujitsu Research of America.

My research focuses on Computer Vision, Foundation Models, and Pre-training Algorithms, with applications in Planetary Science, Earth Observation, and Remote Sensing. I investigate the efficiency of foundation models in relation to spatial data distribution and develop domain-specific foundation models for applications such as Mars science and diverse remote-sensing tasks.

üöÄ Currently, I am developing foundation models for Mars science and investigating how spatial distribution of pre-training data affects geospatial foundation model performance. My work includes creating benchmarks like Mars-Bench and ConeQuest for planetary science applications.

I have extensive research experience spanning Computer Vision, Natural Language Processing, and Speech Processing. During my internships at NASA JPL and Fujitsu Research, I developed benchmarks with 20+ satellite datasets and built super-resolution models for disaster-related satellite imagery by distilling knowledge from geospatial foundation models.
